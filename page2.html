Moderna COVID-19 Vaccine




General Information

Name: mRNA-1273

Manufacturer: ModernaTX, Inc.

Type of vaccine: mRNA

Number of shots: 2 shots, one month (28 days) apart

How given: Shot in the muscle of the upper arm

Does not contain:

  • Eggs
  • Preservatives
  • Latex

Effectiveness

The findings are based on the U.S Food and Drug Administration (FDA)’s data presentation from the late-stage Moderna vaccine trials:

  • 69.5% effective at preventing COVID-19 with symptoms between the first and second dose, with an accurate value between 43.5% and 84.5%. There was a reasonably wide range due to the low number of caught COVID-19s in the trial during the period.
  • The 69.5% figure includes the 13 days before protection starts so that the actual percentage could be higher.
  • About 7% of the Moderna trial volunteers did not attempt the second dose for unknown reasons. The group recorded a 50.8% vaccination efficiency at preventing Covid-19 with symptoms for up to 14 days after the first dose. The efficiency rate increased to 92.1% after 14 days.
  • There was no sufficient statistical evidence to support that the Moderna vaccine can protect one against death and hospitalization.

It was concluded that one would get at least 80% protection against Covid-19 symptoms after Moderna vaccination for 28 days.

Understanding Moderna vaccine technology



Moderna vaccine is an mRNA vaccine, similar to the Pfizer vaccine. In view of this, mRNA vaccines deliver the genetic code to our cell surface, producing protein pieces that can trigger the body’s immune response. The body then produces antibodies and develops longer-lasting immunity against the SARS-CoV-2 virus that might invade the body at the subsequent stage. As such, our immune system, providing natural protection, could identify the extrinsic protein and stimulate the line of body defences toward infection.


Possible Side Effects

Based on the observation during clinical trials, a minority of the study participants experience mild or moderate side effects after vaccination. However, there is insufficient statistical evidence to prove the inherent major safety concerns that would pose a health risk. The common side effects are as listed below:

In the arm where the shot was given:

  • Pain
  • Redness
  • Swelling

In the arm where the shot was given:

  • Tiredness
  • Headache
  • Muscle pain
  • Chills
  • Fever
  • Nausea

Benefits

  • Moderna vaccine can be stored in most standard medical freezers for up to a month, while the Pzifer vaccine can only last for 5 days in the typical refrigerator between 2°C and 8°C.
  • The stability under refrigerated conditions allows for storage at most pharmacies, hospitals, or physicians' offices, comparatively easier to administer to people from rural areas.
  • Once the Moderna vaccine is taken out from the refrigerator for administration, it can remain effective under room temperature conditions for up to 12 hours.
