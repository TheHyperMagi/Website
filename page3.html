The Johnson & Johnson COVID-19 Vaccine




Vaccine Information

Johnson & Johnson is committed to making its COVID-19 vaccine available on a not-for-profit basis for emergency pandemic use. The Company has begun shipping its COVID-19 vaccine and expects to deliver enough single-shot vaccines by the end of March to enable the full vaccination of more than 20 million people in the U.S. The Company plans to deliver 100 million single-shot vaccines to the U.S. during the first half of 2021. Vaccine Administration

The Janssen COVID-19 Vaccine has been tested to prime the immune system using a single dose.

The vaccine uses an adenovirus, a type of virus that causes the common cold - which has been inactivated. The virus will carry a gener from the coronavirus into the human cells, which then produces coronavirus proteins (not the virus itself) to mimic the virus, which primes the immune system to fight off infection if the body encounters the coronavirus. Source : (Johnson & Johnson)

  • Efficacy rate : 66%
  • Cost : 10USD (MYR40) per dose

Research & Testing

45,000 people were fully enrolled in the single-dose clinical trial for the Janssen COVID-19 Vaccine. The trial involved participants of all ages and ethicities, across eight countries and three continents. The study took about 1 month for the team of researchers to study the participants and when they showed peak immunity. Study investigators evaluated participants 28 days after vaccination to asses whether protection had been achieved. With vaccinations, it typically ttakes a few weeks for the immune system to produce enough antibodies against a diesease to convey immunity.


Vaccine Deployment

Given the Johnson & Johnson vaccine is a single dose and has a lower cost, it is expected that it will play an important role in low and middle-income countries.With lower costs and lower requirements of storage and distribution in comparison to the COVID-19 vaccines by Pfizer and Moderna, the Johnson & Johnson vaccine will be more easily transported, stored, and administered.South African health minister Zweli Mkhize announced on 9 February 2021 that the country would sell or swap its 1 million doses of AstraZeneca vaccine. Once it did so, South Africa began vaccination using the Johnson & Johnson vaccine on 17 February 2021, marking the vaccine's first use outside of a clinical trial. Source : Business Insider
